The classroom, rebuilt around how people already work.

Moving from Teams, Outlook, OneDrive and SharePoint to Google Classroom, Gmail and Drive gives pupils and staff one connected place to teach, learn and collaborate. A live link from iSAMS, run by Salamander Soft, keeps it all provisioned automatically so the change is sustainable rather than a one-off scramble.

What students and staff gain

The win is not a different brand of software. It is fewer logins, real collaboration, and class admin that builds itself from the timetable.

For students
  • Real collaboration. Several pupils edit one Doc or Slides deck together, with full version history and no emailed attachments.
  • One place for work. Classroom holds assignments, due dates, feedback and grades, so nothing gets lost between apps.
  • Any device. It runs the same on a phone, a laptop or a low-cost Chromebook, which lowers the cost barrier for families.
  • Built-in support. Read-aloud, captions and translation help pupils with additional needs and EAL learners.
  • One identity. A single sign-on into every class and resource, provisioned automatically from iSAMS.
For staff
  • Classes build themselves. Rosters, classrooms and timetables are created from iSAMS each term, with no manual setup.
  • Faster feedback. Rubrics, comment banks and inline marking in Classroom cut the time around assessment.
  • Plan together. Shared Drives let departments co-own schemes of work instead of passing files around.
  • AI where it helps. Gemini in Education Plus drafts resources, differentiates tasks and speeds up routine writing.
  • Day-one ready, secure by default. Accounts exist on the first morning of term and close automatically when someone leaves.

What the rollout looks like

Staff move a term ahead of pupils, a pilot proves it before the whole school commits, and Microsoft is retired only once Google is carrying the load. The plan runs across an academic year and can compress, but the order should hold.

Discovery and designAutumn 2026
Confirm Education Plus licences and the domain, stand up the Google tenancy, connect Salamander to iSAMS, agree naming conventions, and pick a pilot group of willing staff.
Done when: the tenancy is live, the iSAMS sync is tested, and the pilot group is provisioned.
Staff firstSpring 2027
All staff move to Gmail, Drive and Classroom with a proper training programme, running alongside Teams so nothing breaks. Name a champion in each department.
Done when: staff are confident, key materials are migrated, and every department has a champion.
Pupil pilotSummer 2027
Selected departments or year groups teach through Classroom, guardian summaries go live for those families, and OneDrive and SharePoint content is migrated across.
Done when: the pilot is evaluated, issues are fixed, and parent feedback is positive.
Whole school go-liveSeptember 2027
The senior school teaches on Classroom from day one, with every class auto-provisioned from iSAMS. Firefly is retired and Teams moves into a read-only wind-down.
Done when: all classes are active in Classroom and Firefly is switched off.
Decommission and embedAutumn 2027 on
Close SharePoint and OneDrive, end any Microsoft paid licensing, and review adoption with staff and parents to refine the setup.
Done when: the Microsoft estate is retired and its cost is out of the budget.

Two things that decide whether this lands well

First, sequence staff ahead of pupils without exception. A teacher learning Classroom in the same week they teach through it is the fastest route to a bad first impression. Second, treat data migration as a real workstream, not an afterthought. Years of files in OneDrive and SharePoint need owners, deadlines and a clear cut-off, or content quietly gets stranded on a platform you are trying to switch off.
