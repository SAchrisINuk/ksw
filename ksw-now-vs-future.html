What changes for parents

This is the real return on the move. Today parents juggle several systems and a portal they do not rate. The future gives them one branded front door to everything about their child.

How it feels now
  • Separate logins for Firefly, the iSAMS portal and SOCS
  • An iSAMS parent portal that parents do not enjoy using
  • No single view of a child's day, scattered across systems
  • Pupils' work lives in Teams, with little visibility for parents
How it feels next
  • One username and password for everything
  • Reports, attendance, timetable and exams on a single dashboard
  • Sport fixtures and club sign-ups in the same place
  • Homework visible through Google Classroom guardian summaries

One login, not three

MySchoolPortal aggregates iSAMS and SOCS behind a single branded login, so parents stop hunting across systems for the thing they need.

Their child's day in one view

Reports, attendance, timetables, exam entries and results sit on one dashboard that works the same on a phone, tablet or desktop.

What RGS parents already have

Your nearest competitor runs exactly this pattern, MySchoolPortal over iSAMS and SOCS. The move closes a gap rather than taking a risk.

The honest part

One line in the table carries nearly all the effort, and it is not Firefly.

Teams to Google is the real work, not the real cost

Moving pupils and staff off Microsoft and onto Google is broadly licence-neutral, but it means migrating files and email, retraining staff and changing daily habits across the school. Budget the time and the change management, not just the subscription. Firefly retiring is the easy part. The platform shift is the part that needs a proper plan and a year to land well.
